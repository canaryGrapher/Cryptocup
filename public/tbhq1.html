Question 1

The Indian army is in the midst of an international nuclear high alert. The Indian government has dissolved, but continues to operate underground. There is dire need of safeguarding nuclear codes lest they should get stolen by the enemy. We need to devise a new way to communicate the nuclear codes between the army and the government. The following steps are taken:


  1. The codes (plaintext) is encrypted using a key. The key is put in a square matrix (size=5) followed by {,'a','b','c','d','e','f','g','h','i',[[[NOT 'j']]],'k','l','m',..............'x','y','z'} such that there is no repetition of alphabets in the matrix.

  2. The plaintext is written in terms of the index positions of the letters in the matrix. Another key, the cipher key is also written in terms of the index positions of the letters in the matrix and repeated to reach the length of the plain text.

  3. The ciphertext is formed by adding the plaintext pattern and key pattern.
